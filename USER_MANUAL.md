# Pariprashna · परिप्रश्न - User Manual

## Welcome

**Pariprashna** (परिप्रश्न, *pari = around / comprehensive, prashna = question*) is a community-driven Q&A platform for authentic answers about Hinduism, Sanatan Dharma, and related topics. It features AI-generated answers verified by certified gurus and scholars.

## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. Go to the website and click **"Sign Up"**
2. Fill in your name, email, and password
3. Click **"Sign Up"**
4. You can also sign up with **Google** (click the Google button)

### Logging In

**Regular Users:**
- Click **"Login"** → **"User Login"** tab
- Enter your email and password
- Click **"Login"**

**Gurus/Admins:**
- Click **"Login"** → **"Guru / Admin Login"** tab
- Enter your email and password
- Click **"Login"**

> Note: Only gurus and admins can use the Guru/Admin Login section. Regular users cannot log in through this tab.

---

## Asking Questions

1. Click **"Ask Question"** in the navbar
2. Enter a clear, descriptive title (minimum 15 characters)
3. Optionally describe your question in detail
4. Select tags from the dropdown (at least 1 required)
5. Optionally add a custom tag
6. Click **"Post Your Question"**

**What happens next:**
- Your question is posted immediately
- An AI answer is automatically generated using real shlokas from vedabase.io
- The AI answer is visible to you and shows "🤖 AI Generated - Pending Verification"
- Only admin can verify the AI answer to make it visible to everyone
- Gurus can also post their own answers

---

## Voting

- **Upvote** (orange arrow): Click to upvote, click again to remove
- **Downvote** (red arrow): Click to downvote, click again to remove
- You cannot vote on your own questions/answers
- Voting affects the vote score shown next to posts

---

## Comments

- Click **"Add comment"** on any question or answer
- Type your comment (max 500 characters)
- Click **"Add"** to post

## Tags

## Bounties

### Setting a Bounty
1. Go to your question
2. Click **"⚠️ Set Bounty"**
3. Enter the reputation amount you want to offer
4. Your reputation decreases by that amount

### Awarding a Bounty
1. Go to your question with a bounty
2. Find the best answer
3. Click **"⚠️ Award Bounty"** on that answer
4. The answerer receives your reputation

## Guru Portal

Only gurus, scholars, and admins can access the Guru Portal.

### Verifying Answers
1. Go to **"Guru Portal"** in the navbar
2. View pending answers in the **"Pending Verification"** tab
3. Read the answer carefully
4. Click **"Verify"** and add a verification note
5. The answer moves to **"Verified by You"** tab

### Unverifying Answers
1. Go to **"Verified by You"** tab
2. Click the **✕** button on any verified answer
3. The verification is removed

---

## Admin Panel

Only admins can access the Admin Panel.

### Managing Users
1. Go to **"Admin"** in the navbar
2. View all users in the **"Users"** tab
3. Change user roles using the dropdown

### Verifying AI Answers
1. Go to **"AI Answers"** tab
2. Review the AI-generated answer
3. Click **"Verify"** to approve or **"Reject"** to remove

### Managing Gurus
1. Go to **"Guru Management"** tab
2. Click **"Approve Guru"** to make a user a guru

### Adding / Editing Verified Gurus Manually
1. Go to **"Guru Management"** tab
2. Click **"+ Add Verified Guru"**
3. Fill in:
   - Account fields: full name, email, optional temporary password, user role
   - Profile fields: display name, honorific, photo URL, short bio, full bio
   - Tradition: lineage, sampradaya, tier (scholar / guru / acharya)
   - Knowledge: expertise, scriptures, languages, years of study
   - Public: current position, institution, location, website
   - Admin-only: internal notes
4. Submit. The new guru can log in at `/login` → **Guru / Admin Login** tab.
5. To edit, click **Edit** on the guru's row in the list.
6. To reset the password, click **Reset PW** — a new temporary password is generated.
7. To delete, click **Delete** — you can also delete the linked user account.

> Note: The verified guru pool is intentionally kept small (15–20 experts). Only admins can add new gurus.

## Guru Shloka Reference Syntax

When posting an answer (gurus, scholars, acharyas, admins), you can auto-attach a shloka from the vedabase.io database by typing a reference in the body:

| Pattern | Example | Result |
|---------|---------|--------|
| `@BG <chapter>.<verse>` | `@BG 7.8` | Bhagavad Gita 7.8 |
| `@SB <canto>.<chapter>.<verse>` | `@SB 1.2.3` | Srimad Bhagavatam 1.2.3 |
| `@CC <part> <chapter>.<verse>` | `@CC adi 1.1` | Caitanya-caritamrta Adi 1.1 |
| `@ISO <mantra>` | `@ISO 1` | Sri Isopanishad Mantra 1 |
| `@NOI <verse>` | `@NOI 1` | Nectar of Instruction 1 |

Aliases also work: `GITA`, `BHAGAVATAM`, `BHAGAVATAPURANA`, `CAITANYA`, `ISOPANISHAD`, `NECTAR`.

When the answer is posted, the matching shloka is automatically attached with:
- Original Sanskrit (Devanagari)
- Transliteration (IAST)
- English translation
- Purport (in expandable section)
- Source link to vedabase.io

The attached shlokas are visible to all readers as formatted cards under the answer body.

## Guru Tag Management

On any question detail page, logged-in gurus will see a **"Guru: Manage Tags"** panel with the existing tags. Each tag has a small X button to remove it, and an **+ Add tag** button to add a new tag by name. Adding a tag that doesn't yet exist in the database will create it.

## Guru Question Deletion (Etiquette)

If a question breaks platform etiquette (e.g. not actually about Hinduism, abusive, spam, or factually insulting to a tradition), a guru can remove it:

1. Open the question
2. Click **"Delete as Guru"** in the question header
3. Enter a short reason (e.g. "Not about Hinduism", "Spam", "Insulting to tradition")
4. The question, its answers, and its comments are removed. The reason and the deleting guru are logged for transparency.

| Badge | Meaning |
|-------|---------|

| Scriptural | Quotes or references a scripture accurately |
| Clarification | Asks an important clarifying question |

## FAQ

**Q: Why can't I post answers?**
A: Only gurus, scholars, and admins can post answers. Ask an admin to upgrade your role.

**Q: Why is the AI answer not visible to others?**
A: AI answers need admin verification before they become public. The admin will verify it soon.

**Q: Can I answer my own question?**
A: No, the question asker cannot post answers to their own question.

**Q: How do I become a guru?**
A: Contact the admin. They can upgrade your account to guru status.

**Q: Why does the AI sometimes say "no relevant shloka found"?**
A: The AI only uses verified shlokas from vedabase.io. If no matching shloka exists in our database, it says so honestly instead of making one up.

**Q: How fast is the AI?**
A: The AI responds in about 1 second using Groq's fast inference.

**Q: How does the `@BG 7.8` shloka syntax work?**
A: When a guru (or scholar/acharya/admin) types a shloka reference in the answer body, the system looks it up in the vedabase.io collection and auto-attaches the Sanskrit, transliteration, translation, purport, and source link. The shloka is rendered as a formatted card under the answer.

**Q: Can a guru edit my question or remove tags?**
A: Gurus can add or remove tags on any question to keep categorization accurate. They cannot edit the body of a question, but they can delete the whole question if it breaks platform etiquette.

**Q: Who is a "verified guru"?**
A: Verified gurus are a small, manually-curated group of 15-20 real scholars/teachers added by the admin. They are not self-registered. See the **/gurus** page to see the current pool.
